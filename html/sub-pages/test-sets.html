Comparative Modeling Test Sets

  • Homology-derived models, Azat Badretdinov's comparative models for proteins of known structure, organized in groups of different accuracy; they were used for testing statistical potentials.

  • GOOD and BAD comparative models. Models for proteins of known structure, organized in a GOOD group containing models based on correct templates and approximately correct alignments, and BAD group containing models based on incorrect templates or very poor alignments. These model sets have been used in the following publications:

    • Sánchez and Sali, Large-scale protein structure modeling of the Saccharomyces cerevisiae genome, Proc. Natl. Acad. Sci. USA, 95, 13597-13602, 1998.


    • Melo, F., Sánchez, R., and Sali, A. Statistical potentials for fold assessment, Protein Science, 11, 430-448, 2002.
