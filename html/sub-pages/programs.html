Programs, databases and servers from the Sali Lab




  • MODELLER, a program for comparative protein structure modelling by satisfaction of spatial restraints.

    •  
  • ModBase, a database of sequence-structure alignments and evaluated 3D models from large scale comparative protein structure modeling of the S. cerevisiae genome.

    •  
  • DBAli, a database of sequence alignments that contains more than 35,000 pairwise structural alignments generated using different alignment methods.

    •  
  • AlBase-3, a database of multiple protein structure alignments used in the paper by A. Sali and J.P. Overington. Derivation of rules for comparative protein modeling from a database of protein structure alignments. Protein Science 3, 1582-1596, 1994.

    •  
  • Comparative Modeling Test Sets. Sets of comparative models used for assessing evaluation methods.
  • Loop modeling test sets for assessing modeling techniques of short segments.

    •  
  • Models from publications.

    •  
  • pG server, a server for evaluation of comparative protein structure models by MODELLER based on a ProsaII Z-score.

    •  
  • ASGL, a program for PostScript plotting (curves, histograms, density plots, ...) from simple data files, including those generated by MODELLER.

    •  
  • COMBASE, a database of protein-protein complexes.

    •  
  • Miscellaneous programs, PSA, ELLIPSOID, ...
