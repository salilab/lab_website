Andrej Sali Lab

 Laboratory of Molecular Biophysics
Pels Family Center for Biochemistry and Structural Biology

Research Area
We are developing and applying methods for protein structure modeling, primarily in the area of homology or comparative modeling. In addition, we began a project in structural genomics by exhaustively calculating three-dimensional models for proteins encoded in whole genomes, and exploring new ways of using these models.

Theories of Protein Folding and Protein Structure Modeling
Homology protein structure modeling is based on sequence similarity between a structurally undefined target sequence and its structurally defined homologs. Homology modeling involves searching protein sequence and structure databases, aligning sequences with structures, modeling core regions, loops and sidechains, evaluating models and developing statistical energy functions. These techniques, currently used to model as many as one-third of the proteins in various genomes, will ultimately lead to expanded structure-based functional characterization of proteins and thus enhance the impact of various genome sequencing projects on biology and medicine.
